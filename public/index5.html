Chichen Itza
Mexico

Chichen Itza, a sprawling ancient city in Mexico's Yucatan Peninsula, is a key site of the Maya civilization. Founded around 600 AD, it flourished between the 10th and 12th centuries as a major center of political, economic, and cultural activity. The site is famous for its impressive architecture, including the Pyramid of Kukulcán, also known as El Castillo. This step pyramid is renowned for its astronomical significance; during the equinox, shadows cast by the sun create the illusion of a serpent descending the pyramid’s steps. Other notable structures include the Great Ball Court, the Temple of the Warriors, and the Sacred Cenote, a natural well used for ceremonial purposes. Rediscovered in the 19th century, Chichen Itza is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and draws millions of visitors annually. Its recognition as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World underscores its immense historical and cultural importance, reflecting the advanced achievements of the Maya civilization.
