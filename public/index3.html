Christ the Redeemer
Location: Brasil

Christ the Redeemer, an iconic symbol of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, stands majestically atop the Corcovado Mountain. This colossal statue of Jesus Christ, completed in 1931, reaches a height of 98 feet and extends 82 feet across its outstretched arms, making it one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. Designed by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, the statue was constructed using reinforced concrete and soapstone. Christ the Redeemer was created as a symbol of peace and reconciliation, reflecting Brazil's diverse cultural heritage and spiritual values. The statue overlooks the city of Rio de Janeiro and the surrounding bay, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the landscape. As a global icon, it attracts millions of tourists each year who come to marvel at its grandeur and the spectacular vistas it provides. In 2007, it was named one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, further solidifying its place in global cultural heritage.
