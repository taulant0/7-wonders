Great Wall of China
Location: China

The Great Wall of China, a monumental feat of ancient engineering, stretches over 13,000 miles across northern China. Built over several dynasties, including the Qin, Han, and Ming, its primary purpose was to protect against invasions and raids from various nomadic groups that roamed the northern frontier. Constructed from a variety of materials such as earth, wood, and stone, the Wall is not a single continuous structure but rather a series of walls and fortifications that vary significantly in height and width. In addition to its defensive role, the Great Wall also served as a symbol of the strength, unity, and determination of the Chinese empire. Watchtowers and military outposts were strategically placed along its length to facilitate communication and enhance defensive capabilities. Today, the Great Wall stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a global icon of China's rich historical and cultural heritage. It continues to draw millions of visitors annually who come to marvel at its grandeur and historical significance.
