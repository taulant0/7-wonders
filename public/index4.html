Machu Picchu
Location: Peru

Machu Picchu, an ancient Incan city nestled high in the Peruvian Andes, is one of the most enigmatic and breathtaking archaeological sites in the world. Built in the 15th century under the Inca emperor Pachacuti, this remarkable citadel sits approximately 8,000 feet above sea level. Known for its sophisticated dry-stone construction, Machu Picchu features terraced fields, temples, and palaces intricately integrated into the mountainous landscape. Key structures include the Temple of the Sun and the Intihuatana stone, believed to have been used for astronomical and religious purposes. Rediscovered by American historian Hiram Bingham in 1911, Machu Picchu has since captivated global audiences with its mystery and architectural brilliance. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, it attracts millions of visitors annually who come to explore its historical significance and stunning natural beauty. Its recognition as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World underscores its extraordinary cultural and historical importance.
