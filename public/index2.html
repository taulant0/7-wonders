Petra
Location: Jordan

Petra, Jordan, is a stunning archaeological marvel renowned for its rock-cut architecture and vibrant history. Carved into the red sandstone cliffs of southern Jordan, this ancient city dates back to around the 4th century BC and was once a thriving trading hub of the Nabataean Kingdom. Petra's elaborate network of tombs, temples, and monuments showcases the advanced engineering and artistic skills of its builders. The city's location along key trade routes allowed it to flourish as a cultural and economic center, influencing the region significantly. Rediscovered by Western explorers in the early 19th century, Petra is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. It attracts millions of visitors each year who come to explore its breathtaking historical and architectural splendor.
